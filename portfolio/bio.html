I’m Cooper Shaw, a first year Computer Engineering major at Georgia Tech.

I hate to admit it, but I’m only one small coincidence away from not being able to say that.

Not many first year Georgia Tech students have the unfortunate privilege of saying this, but I was rejected from Georgia Tech.

On Thursday, May 25th, 2023, I received an email from Georgia Tech admissions saying, “At this point, our incoming first-year class is full and we are not able to offer you admission.”

On Friday, May 26th, 2023, the day after I was rejected from the waitlist, I received a second email saying, “Congratulations! We are happy to offer you admission to Georgia Tech's 2023 first-year class for the Fall term beginning on Monday, August 21, 2023.”

How did I reverse this decision in the span of just a day? By getting absurdly lucky.

Georgia Tech offers guaranteed admission to Valedictorian and Salutatorians of all public Georgia high schools. By the thinnest of margins, I was Salutatorian of Marietta High school.

When I say “the thinnest of margins,” I mean it. My GPA was 0.01 points higher than 3rd place, and 0.02 higher than 4th. I couldn’t have cut it closer.

And this wasn’t by design. I didn’t mean to be Salutatorian. Granted, in freshman year, I could be quoted as saying “I might be Valedictorian or Salutatorian,” but this wasn’t a vow that I strove to achieve. Instead, it was just an observation of my dedication to school for dedication’s sake; I really didn’t care whether I was top-ranked–I did it because that's just who I am.

What’s even more absurd is the fact that I was forced into taking eight 5.0-level classes Senior year. I just happened to have a weird set of courses that would have left me with free blocks in the middle of the day, If I had taken different classes, I wouldn’t have been Salutatorian, and I would be enrolled at UGA right now. The entire course of my life would have been different.

While my enrollment at Georgia Tech is due to a series of unlikely events, there’s an important thing to remember–I was only in position for those coincidences to occur because of the hard work I was already doing. I put myself in a position to be successful.

While I didn’t know my approach to life could be so beneficial at the time, I definitely know it now. I understand that hard work can pay off, and you’re much more likely to get lucky if you set yourself up for success.

Right now, I’m setting myself up for success by working hard in college, focusing on my internship at Tenovos, and independently learning outside of what is required of me.
