< short term goals >

Growing up, I was never challenged by school. As similar situations did for many people before me, this resulted in me having no idea how to study.

Over the past four months, I’ve been doing my best to teach myself how to learn.

I’ve started scheduling out time to study, using a Pomodoro timer, and putting my phone away when I’m working.

I’ve definitely started studying more efficiently, but I’m still not where I want to be academically.

The way I see it, if I can get really good at studying, the benefits will be twofold. First, the obvious one: I’ll get better grades. Secondly, though, this would free up so much free time that I can use to better myself. If I am able to get my act together, I want to use my free time to get better at programming, teach myself about computer hardware, and put energy into projects that I’m proud of.

< long term goals >

My biggest inspiration growing up was my uncle, Brian Hook. He has worked on the Quake engine, the Oculus VR headsets, and was the director of a team of engineers at Facebook for a number of years.

His level of talent has afforded him some handsome salaries and benefits, a portion of which has trickled down to me in the form of Christmas gifts.

The Christmas and birthday gifts I received from him reshaped the course of my childhood–he allowed me to explore a wonderful variety of passions that may have otherwise been prohibitively expensive.

Importantly, though, his jobs had been fun. He’s not a lawyer or an accountant (which I imagine would be very boring); he got to play with gadgets and gizmos and make games.

That’s what I want. I not only get to be professionally fulfilled, but I will be able to fund and facilitate not only my own passions, but also those of my loved ones.

To pursue this, I’m starting with internships over the course of the next four years. I’m currently an intern at a tech startup called Tenovos. In the past, I have done documentation writing for them, but I’m starting as a software engineering intern next semester.

Beyond this current internship, I want to branch out into a variety of internships before I graduate so I can get familiar with a range of work environments, learn skills, find what I really love to do, and bolster my resume.

Even longer term, my absolute dream is to research computer architecture and develop cutting edge computing technology. Alternatively, I would also love to develop hardware for a company like Apple or Google.

< career goals >

In order to be successful in the tech industry, I need to better myself now. I hope to graduate from Georgia Tech in Spring 2027, so before then I want to get a variety of internships during my college experience, learn how to work more efficiently, and pursue extraneous skills that make me more capable and hireable. I expect to work various jobs in tech as I gain skills and become exceptional in my field.
